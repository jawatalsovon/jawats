About Me

Hello there,
This is Jawat Al Sovon. I am a high school graduate from Rajshahi Cadet College, one of the most prestigious institutions in my country. I love to dream, And itâ€™s always gratifying to see some of my dreams come true, like earning my own money to buy presents for my family and performing on a major stage.
But one of my earliest dreams remains unfulfilled. In fifth grade, while studying a paragraph on "Unemployment issues in Bangladesh," I first imagined that I could one day address this challenge.
A few years ago, I met Taukir Vai, a freelancer who started his own IT firm after he couldn't get into a public university and was ridiculed for his academic "failure". However, now, he's training others and building a skilled workforce. However, due to his lack of official recognition, his firm's growth remains limited. So I found that to tackle a problem on a large scale, only initiative and practical skills are not enough; one has to have an accredited education.

I've found my passion in data science, where I love dealing with datasets and using them to dig out real solutions. A data science major from a worldwide-recognized university will provide me with practical expertise and the official credibility I need. By the time I graduate, I plan to establish my own data firm. I want to build it into a company, maybe even a multinational one someday, creating opportunities for at least a portion of the 2.6 million unemployed people in my country, including 1.38 million unemployed college graduates.
